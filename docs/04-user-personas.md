# 04 User Personas: Zeepro Website

## Purpose
To define the primary audience segments that will interact with the Zeepro website. By understanding their goals, frustrations, and digital behaviors, the UX, design, and copywriting teams can tailor the website to directly address their needs and establish trust.

## Scope
This document covers the B2B personas relevant to the v1 informational website launch. It deliberately excludes direct-to-consumer (B2C) homeowner personas, as they fall outside the current operational scope defined by the business requirements.

## Assumptions
- **Assumption 1: Validation-Seeking Behavior.** We assume that most users arrive at the website after hearing about Zeepro through word-of-mouth or offline networking.
  - **Justification:** Without an established digital marketing footprint, the website functions primarily as a validation tool. Users are looking to confirm that Zeepro is legitimate, professional, and capable of handling their specific project scale before making formal contact.
- **Assumption 2: Time-Sensitivity.** We assume the personas are highly busy professionals who skim content rather than reading deeply.
  - **Justification:** B2B decision-makers in the UAE construction sector manage multiple projects simultaneously. The design must favor bullet points, bold typography, and clear calls-to-action (CTAs) over long paragraphs.

## Known Facts
- **Target Segments:** The audience consists of Real Estate Brokers, Main Contractors, and Facility Managers.
- **Geographic Context:** All personas operate within the fast-paced UAE market, meaning they expect rapid responses and high standards of corporate presentation.
- **Communication Preference:** Direct WhatsApp communication is heavily favored over traditional email for initial, informal inquiries in this demographic.

## Unknown Information
- **Detailed Demographics:** Are the primary decision-makers typically expats or local Emiratis? (This affects language preferences and cultural tone).
- **Primary Pain Point:** What is the single biggest reason these personas abandon their current subcontractors/maintenance providers? (e.g., poor communication, low quality, delays?).

## Persona Profiles (v1)

### Persona 1: The Real Estate Broker ("Tariq")
- **Role:** Senior Broker at a mid-sized Dubai real estate agency.
- **Goal:** Needs a reliable renovation and maintenance partner to quickly prep properties for new tenants or buyers.
- **Frustration:** Subcontractors who delay timelines, costing him commission and angering property owners.
- **Website Need:** Wants to quickly see proof of quality, confirm Zeepro handles both minor maintenance and major renovations, and find a direct WhatsApp number to send a quick property video for a rough estimate.

### Persona 2: The Main Contractor ("Sarah")
- **Role:** Project Manager at a large UAE construction firm.
- **Goal:** Needs dependable subcontractors to handle overflow work or specialized phases (e.g., finishing, MEP) of larger projects.
- **Frustration:** Subcontractors who lack proper safety compliance, trade licenses, or sufficient manpower.
- **Website Need:** Looking for "trust signals"—trade licenses, safety certifications, company history, and a professional corporate profile. She will use the official email form to request a formal capability statement.

### Persona 3: The Facility Manager ("Ahmed")
- **Role:** Operations Manager for a portfolio of commercial office buildings.
- **Goal:** Seeking a long-term, reliable partner for ongoing preventative and reactive maintenance.
- **Frustration:** Inconsistent service quality and slow response times to emergency repair calls.
- **Website Need:** Needs to clearly understand the scope of "Maintenance" services offered. Looking for professionalism, reliability guarantees, and clear contact escalation paths.
